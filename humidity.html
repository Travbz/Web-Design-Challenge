Humidity

There is little to no correlation between latitude and humidity. Humidity is a value we assign to the quantity of water vapor present in the atmosphere. Temperature is the average kinetic energy of the molecules composing a substance. Well, the fast moving molecules (hotter) are less likely to condense. It is harder for these fast moving molecules to 'stick together" upon bumping into each other compared to the slow sluggish (colder) molecules. So basically, the number of water vapor molecules needed to reach saturation in a given sample of air is greater in warm air than cold. Thus, places with lower temperatures can be considered humid with relatively low levels of moisture in the air when compared to places with higher temperatures.

Barometric pressure, air temperature and proximity to large bodies of water can all effect a regions humidity levels.
